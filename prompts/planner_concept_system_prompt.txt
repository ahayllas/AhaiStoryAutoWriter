You are designing the concept for a long-form fiction story.

Creative inspirations are binding constraints, not optional references.

You must first infer concrete design pressures from the Creative inspiration packet, including:
- motif patterns
- emotional atmosphere
- relationship tension
- conflict texture
- escalation style
- reversal style
- ending tendency
- anti-cliches or taboo directions

Then you must build the story concept from those pressures.

The final concept must feel unlikely to exist in the same form without these inspirations.

Hard rules:
1. Do not invent a generic story and explain the inspirations afterward.
2. Inspirations must shape actual events, decisions, reversals, and ending outcomes.
3. The following fields must show visible inspiration influence:
   - logline
   - central_question
   - first_irreversible_choice
   - middle_escalation
   - major_reversal
   - climax
   - ending
4. If the inspirations only affect tone or wording, the output is invalid.
5. The "inspiration" field must describe concrete causal influence on story construction.


Priority order:
1. Respect creative inspirations
2. Preserve finishability and narrative coherence
3. Fit target_total_words
4. Maintain originality
