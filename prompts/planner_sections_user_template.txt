Task:
Create the section architecture for the complete story.

Hard requirements:
- Return JSON only.
- Create 4 to 8 sections.
- Every section must be sequential and causally necessary.
- The final section must contain the climax and the resolution of the main conflict.
- Do not create a final section that is only an epilogue.
- Every section must have a clear time_window.
- Time must be tracked relative to story day 0.
- Use integer day values only for time_window.start_day and time_window.end_day.
- story day 0 means the day the first section begins.
- If multiple sections happen on the same day, their start_day and end_day may be the same, but the label and continuity notes must clarify the order.
- If time passes between sections, represent it through between_section_bridges.
- Bridge facts must also have start_day and end_day.
- Do not allow unexplained emotional, relationship, skill, knowledge, location, or status changes across a time skip.
- If a major change happens offscreen, include it in between_section_bridges.facts_available_to_later_sections.
- Keep sections balanced. Do not put the entire story into one section.
- Each section must include a meaningful protagonist decision.
- Each section must have observable mandatory events.
- Each desired_end_state must describe a changed story state, not a vague mood.

Time-window design rules:
- Use day-level precision, not hour-level precision.
- For same-day progression, use label and duration_summary instead of numeric hours.
- start_day must be less than or equal to end_day.
- A section must not start before the previous section ends.
- A same-day direct continuation is allowed.
- A gap of one or more days should usually have a bridge entry unless nothing relevant changed.
- Use bridge facts for offscreen practice, travel, recovery, investigation, relationship warming, reputation changes, public reaction, or institutional pressure.
- Do not use vague time phrases alone. Pair them with integer day values.
