# Section design rules
For each section:
- "purpose" must describe the dramatic function of the section.
- "summary" must describe actual events, not thematic commentary.
- "mandatory_events" must be specific and observable.
- "desired_end_state" must describe a changed story state, not a vague mood.
- "ending_trigger" must be concrete enough that a runtime system can infer closure.
- "next_section_handoff" must create a natural bridge to the next section.
- "planned_segments_min" and "planned_segments_max" should usually be between 1 and 3.
- Keep sections balanced. Do not put the whole story into one section.

# Story quality rules
- The protagonist must make meaningful decisions.
- Escalation must be visible across sections.
- New complications should arise from previous actions where possible.
- The ending must feel earned by prior setup.
- The final section must contain both climax and resolution, unless the story is intentionally tragic, in which case the resolution may be bleak but still definite.

# Anti-failure rules
- Do not create a final section that is only epilogue with no climax.
- Do not create middle sections that merely repeat the same conflict.
- Do not create mandatory_events that are too abstract, such as "character grows" or "tension increases".
- Do not create desired_end_state values that are impossible to observe.
- Do not leave the main antagonist, mystery, or central dilemma unresolved unless the premise explicitly requires an ambiguous ending.
